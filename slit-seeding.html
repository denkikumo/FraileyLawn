Why slit seeding works

Better seed-to-soil contact gives new grass a stronger start.

Broadcasting seed across the surface can leave much of it sitting on top of thatch or existing grass. Slit seeding places seed into shallow grooves at a more consistent depth, helping protect it while improving access to moisture and soil.

Direct soil contact

Seed is placed into narrow grooves instead of resting loosely on top of the lawn.

More even coverage

Professional equipment distributes seed consistently across thin, worn or damaged areas.

Ideal for lawn renovation

Slit seeding can restore a tired lawn without removing the entire existing turf.

Choosing the right process

Slit seeding, overseeding and core aeration are not the same.

Each process solves a different problem. The best choice depends on lawn density, soil condition, thatch buildup and the amount of repair needed.

Slit seeding

Cuts planting grooves and places seed directly into the soil. It is especially useful for thin lawns, bare areas and broader lawn renovation.

Overseeding

Spreads seed over existing turf. It can improve density, but results depend heavily on whether the seed reaches exposed soil.

Core aeration

Removes plugs of soil to reduce compaction and improve movement of air, water and nutrients. It does not plant seed at a controlled depth.

Frequently asked questions

What to know before scheduling slit seeding

What is the difference between slit seeding, overseeding and core aeration?

Slit seeding cuts narrow grooves and places seed directly into them for strong seed-to-soil contact. Overseeding spreads seed over an existing lawn without cutting planting grooves. Core aeration removes plugs of soil to reduce compaction and improve air and water movement, but it does not place seed at a controlled depth.

What is the best time to seed a lawn in Central Ohio?

Late summer through early fall is generally the strongest window because the soil is still warm while air temperatures begin to cool. Frailey Lawn Renewal is currently booking fall seeding from mid-August through September.

How long does slit seeding take to show results?

Visible germination commonly begins within about 14–21 days under normal conditions when the seed receives consistent moisture. Establishment time varies with grass type, weather, soil conditions and watering.

How should I water after slit seeding?

Keep the seeded soil consistently moist with light, frequent watering and do not allow it to dry out during germination. Exact written watering instructions are provided after service. Following that schedule is required for the germination guarantee.
